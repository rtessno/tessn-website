Trust and deployment

State what is controlled, what is directional, and what remains unverified.

This preview separates demonstrated website behavior, Current product principles, pilot-specific commitments, and claims that require additional evidence or review.

Website behavior

A static preview with deliberately limited data handling.

The public repository contains presentation source and governance documentation. The site does not operate the Current product or accept customer evidence.

STATIC

No application accounts

The preview has no sign-in, customer workspace, payment flow, or product runtime.

NO INTAKE

No direct form collection

No embedded pilot form, mailing list, analytics service, or evidence-upload workflow is enabled.

NO DOWNLOADS

Release files remain separate

No installer is published while signing, checksums, licensing, support, and release governance remain incomplete.

Current principles

Evidence and reviewability before confident automation.

These statements describe the product approach. Individual deployment, integration, security, and operational controls must be demonstrated for the exact release and pilot configuration being evaluated.

Inspectable evidence

Investigation findings and outputs are intended to remain connected to supporting material rather than becoming unsupported summaries.

Deterministic analysis first

Structured tools and explicit investigation records should precede optional generative explanation.

Human review remains necessary

Current is not presented as autonomous diagnosis or a guarantee of root cause, completeness, or error-free conclusions.

Deployment is configuration-specific

Hosting, storage, external AI use, integrations, access, retention, and support boundaries must be documented for the evaluated environment.

Claims not made

Absence of a claim is an intentional trust boundary.

The preview does not convert roadmap direction, test coverage, or founder intent into certifications or commercial guarantees.

  • No certification claimNo security certification, compliance attestation, or regulatory readiness is represented.
  • No availability claimNo general-availability, uptime, support-response, or production-scale customer claim is published.
  • No outcome guaranteeNo percentage improvement, faster-resolution promise, reduced-cost claim, or guaranteed diagnosis is stated.
  • No integration assumptionA named integration must be demonstrable in the exact release before it is presented as available.
  • No legal-entity inferenceTessn is an umbrella brand and is not presented as a formed LLC.
  • No installer assuranceNo signed, notarized, scanned, or supported installer is offered through this preview.

Pilot boundary

Interest intake is not evidence transfer.

A first conversation should use high-level professional and workflow information. Customer evidence or sensitive technical material requires a separate written process.

START

Synthetic or approved data

Initial demonstrations and product visuals use synthetic data by default.

AGREE

Document boundaries first

Deployment, approved data, access, retention, integrations, support, and evidence transfer must be agreed before a live pilot.

LIMIT

Do not send sensitive evidence

Initial outreach must not include logs, tickets, captures, credentials, internal URLs, proprietary source, regulated information, contracts, or pricing.

Release and publication controls

The website and release repository are separate. Public software distribution remains disabled until the release process defines supported platforms, signing and notarization, malware scanning, checksums, software-bill-of-materials handling, licensing, upgrade and rollback expectations, support boundaries, and issue reporting.

Search indexing and custom-domain metadata also remain disabled until naming, domain, content, privacy, legal, accessibility, and launch gates are complete.

This page describes current preview boundaries and product principles. It is not a security assessment, compliance statement, service-level agreement, data-processing agreement, pilot agreement, or software warranty.
