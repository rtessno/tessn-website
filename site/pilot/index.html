Design-partner program

Evaluate Current against real escalation work.

The initial pilot is intended to validate whether a structured investigation workspace improves evidence handling, handoffs, reproducibility, and knowledge capture for technical support teams.

Pilot fit

A focused engagement—not an open-ended software trial.

The strongest design partner handles recurring complex escalations, can identify a small group of technical users, and is willing to compare the Current workflow against an existing baseline.

TEAM

Technical support environment

Tier 2, Tier 3, escalation, supportability, or customer reliability work.

PROBLEM

Fragmented investigation evidence

Logs, tickets, commands, files, notes, screenshots, and handoffs live across multiple tools.

COMMITMENT

Measurable participation

A defined workflow, a limited participant group, and candid operational feedback.

Proposed engagement

What the pilot is designed to include.

Commercial terms, duration, deployment, support boundaries, and data handling must be finalized in a written pilot agreement before a customer engagement begins.

Discovery and baseline

Document the current escalation workflow, evidence sources, handoff method, and measurable friction.

Controlled setup

Define deployment boundaries, user access, approved data, integrations, and support expectations.

Guided investigations

Run a limited set of representative cases through the Current workflow with founder support.

Outcome review

Compare the workflow against agreed signals such as preparation effort, evidence traceability, handoff quality, and repeated analysis.

Decision

Document what worked, what failed, what must be hardened, and whether a commercial continuation makes sense.
