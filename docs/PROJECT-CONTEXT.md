# Tessn Website Project Context

## Purpose

This repository exists to provide a professional public presentation layer for Tessn and Current without exposing the private Current development repository.

## Brand structure

- **Tessn** — provisional umbrella brand
- **Current** — provisional product name pending final clearance
- **Tessn Solutions LLC** — possible future legal entity name; do not use as a statement of present legal status

The parent-company and product-name decisions remain separable. The website should emphasize Current while using Tessn as the quiet umbrella.

## Product thesis

Current is the support investigation platform for complex technical escalations. It turns fragmented evidence—tickets, logs, commands, files, notes, events, screenshots, and documentation—into structured, reproducible, and auditable investigations.

## Initial market wedge

Primary users:

- Tier 2 and Tier 3 support engineers
- Escalation engineers
- Supportability engineers
- Customer reliability engineers
- Support-focused technical specialists

Primary environments:

- B2B software
- Cloud infrastructure
- Developer tools
- Enterprise platforms
- Technical managed services

Primary buyers and champions:

- Support engineering managers
- Directors and heads of technical support
- Senior escalation owners
- Support operations leaders

## Positioning guardrails

Lead with the investigation problem and operational outcomes. Do not lead with generic labels such as AI assistant, copilot, all-in-one platform, or single pane of glass.

Do not promise:

- autonomous or guaranteed diagnosis
- replacement of the customer's support stack
- measured time or cost improvements without validated pilot evidence
- security, compliance, deployment, or integration capabilities that cannot be demonstrated in the target release

## Website objective

The website must let a visitor understand within a few minutes:

1. What Current is.
2. Why complex support investigations outgrow ticket comments.
3. How Current structures evidence and reasoning.
4. Why the product is different from ticketing, observability, chat, wikis, and generic AI.
5. Who the pilot is for.
6. How to express interest once a contact channel is configured.

## Current business status

- Company formation remains undecided.
- No public installer is offered through this site.
- The release repository is separate: `rtessno/current-release`.
- Pilot pricing, contracts, support boundaries, and data-handling terms remain to be finalized.
- The website source repository was observed as public on July 21, 2026 even though the founder intended it to be private. Repository visibility must be verified manually.

## Non-goals for this phase

- User accounts
- Payment processing
- Customer evidence upload
- SaaS hosting for Current
- Public installer distribution
- Marketing analytics
- Newsletter automation
- Unverified customer logos or testimonials
